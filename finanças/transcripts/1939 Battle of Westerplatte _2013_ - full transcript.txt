Westerplatte - a peninsula located at the mouth of the Port of Gdansk. A small piece of land, 2 km long by 500 m wide. In the nineteenth century, it served as a holiday resort, with a beach and a restaurant. As a result of the Treaty of Versailles, ending World War I, land that once belonged to the Third Reich became the Free City of Gdansk. Westerplatte was later granted exclusively to Poland.
