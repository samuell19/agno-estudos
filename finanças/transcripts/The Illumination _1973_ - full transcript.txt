Illumination is a term and a notion of Augustine's. The term and the notion itself were present throughout the Middle Ages, in all medieval philosophy. According to this doctrine we obtain knowledge by the enlightenment of the mind. Then, in the very moment of enlightenment, the mind sees the truth directly, just as our eyes see the physical world. The mind sees it directly, without reasoning. Illumination is not a result of ecstasy; it's not thoughtless ecstasy or enrapture. It's the heightening of thought. The only thing necessary to achieve this state is purity of heart. Purity of heart is more important than the action of the mind.
