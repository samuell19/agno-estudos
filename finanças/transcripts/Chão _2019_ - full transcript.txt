Brazil is the fifth most unequal country in terms of access to land. Only 1% of rural properties concentrate about 45% of all arable land. Most of it is occupied by agrobusiness, and many areas remain unproductive. Despite being responsible for over 70% of food production in Brazil, family agriculture occupies a small part of the territory.
